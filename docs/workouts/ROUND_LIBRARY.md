# ROUND_LIBRARY.md — Reusable Round Archetypes

A round archetype is a **proven building block** — a round with a known purpose, teaching objective, and coach behaviour. Authors assemble workouts from these instead of inventing every round from scratch, which is how different coaches produce workouts that all feel like Corner.

Each archetype defines: **Purpose · Typical duration · Teaching objective · Common mistakes (to cue against) · Coach behaviour.** Durations assume standard (intermediate) timing; scale per `PROGRESSION_MODEL.md`. Cue wording comes from `CUE_LIBRARY.md`.

> Archetypes are starting points, not straitjackets. Combine and adapt them — but keep each round to **one focus**.

---

## A. Punch-development archetypes

### Single Jab
- **Purpose:** the foundation — establish range, rhythm, and a sharp, straight lead hand.
- **Duration:** 120–180s.
- **Teaching objective:** a jab that snaps out and back on the same line, from a balanced stance.
- **Common mistakes:** looping the jab, dropping the rear hand, leaning in, no retraction.
- **Coach behaviour:** calm, patient, range-focused. Cues on the line of the jab, retraction ("hand back"), and stance. Sparse — let them find the rhythm.

### Double Jab
- **Purpose:** learn to layer the jab — set up, blind, and close distance with two.
- **Duration:** 120–180s.
- **Teaching objective:** two jabs on the same line, the second sharper, feet following.
- **Common mistakes:** the second jab drifting, stopping the feet, telegraphing.
- **Coach behaviour:** rhythmic ("one… one-two"), reinforce the second jab's snap and stepping in behind it.

### Jab and Move
- **Purpose:** marry the jab to footwork — hit and don't be there.
- **Duration:** 150–180s.
- **Teaching objective:** jab, then immediately move off-line (angle or step out); never stand admiring.
- **Common mistakes:** flat feet after the jab, moving straight back, forgetting the hands on the exit.
- **Coach behaviour:** movement-forward, tempo-driven. "Jab — and move." Emphasize the exit and hands-up-while-moving.

### Straight Right (Cross)
- **Purpose:** develop the power hand behind the jab.
- **Duration:** 150–180s.
- **Teaching objective:** rotate the hip and pivot the rear foot; sit down on the cross; recover the guard.
- **Common mistakes:** arm-punching (no hip), lifting the head, over-committing balance, dropping the lead hand.
- **Coach behaviour:** power-and-mechanics focus. Cue hip rotation, foot pivot, and recovery. Moderate density.

### Lead Hook
- **Purpose:** add the hook — the first real angle of attack.
- **Duration:** 150–180s.
- **Teaching objective:** turn the hook from the hips/feet, correct elbow height, tight and short.
- **Common mistakes:** swinging wide/arm-only, dropping the opposite hand, no pivot.
- **Coach behaviour:** mechanics-first, tight-and-turn language, guard reminders on the open side.

### Rear Uppercut
- **Purpose:** add the inside/vertical shot — the body and the guard-splitter.
- **Duration:** 150–180s.
- **Teaching objective:** bend the knees and drive up; short and vertical; don't drop the hand to load.
- **Common mistakes:** loading low (telegraph/exposure), all arm, standing too tall.
- **Coach behaviour:** drive-from-the-legs cues; warn against loading; keep it short.

## B. Movement & defense archetypes

### Footwork
- **Purpose:** the engine of boxing — balance, range control, angles, staying light.
- **Duration:** 120–180s.
- **Teaching objective:** move on the balls of the feet, keep the stance, cut angles, never cross the feet.
- **Common mistakes:** flat-footed, crossing feet, standing square, moving straight in/out only.
- **Coach behaviour:** light, rhythmic, spatial. Cue angles and staying balanced; low cue density, high movement.

### Defence
- **Purpose:** learn not to get hit — slips, rolls, guard, and resetting.
- **Duration:** 150–180s.
- **Teaching objective:** tight defensive mechanics (slip small, roll under, hands home) and immediate reset.
- **Common mistakes:** over-slipping/losing balance, dropping hands to move the head, freezing after defending.
- **Coach behaviour:** deliberate and safety-minded. One mechanic at a time; always cue the reset. Calm energy.

### Counter Punching
- **Purpose:** defend *and* answer — turn defense into offense.
- **Duration:** 150–180s.
- **Teaching objective:** slip/roll then fire the counter (e.g. slip jab → cross); the answer follows instantly.
- **Common mistakes:** defending then pausing, countering off-balance, over-thinking the sequence.
- **Coach behaviour:** tactical, timing-focused. Pair the defense and the counter in one call; reinforce the instant answer.

## C. Conditioning & fight-context archetypes

### Conditioning
- **Purpose:** build work capacity — output and endurance over polish.
- **Duration:** 150–180s, tighter rest.
- **Teaching objective:** sustained volume with acceptable form under fatigue; keep breathing.
- **Common mistakes:** blowing out early, form collapsing, holding the breath, dropping the guard when tired.
- **Coach behaviour:** sparse cues, output-driven. About *work*, not correction. Breathing and pace cues; encouragement earned late.

### Fight Pace
- **Purpose:** train at the tempo of a real round — bursts and recovery within the round.
- **Duration:** 180s.
- **Teaching objective:** vary intensity like a bout — flurry, reset, flurry; stay composed at pace.
- **Common mistakes:** one flat pace, panic-punching, forgetting defense at speed.
- **Coach behaviour:** dynamic energy that surges and settles. Calls flurries and resets; keeps composure cues in.

### Pressure
- **Purpose:** learn to walk an opponent down — cut the ring, stay behind the jab, relentless.
- **Duration:** 180s.
- **Teaching objective:** step in behind punches, cut angles to trap, maintain guard while advancing.
- **Common mistakes:** rushing in straight, dropping the guard to advance, arm-punching while moving forward.
- **Coach behaviour:** forward, insistent, tactical. Cue cutting the ring and hands-up-while-pressing. Rising intensity.

### Fight Simulation (composite)
- **Purpose:** the immersive round — mixed combinations, defensive exchanges, pace changes, a real finish.
- **Duration:** 180s (usually the peak/late round).
- **Teaching objective:** put it all together under a bout-like flow.
- **Common mistakes:** losing structure, gassing from over-pace, abandoning defense.
- **Coach behaviour:** corner-during-a-bout energy — the coach's biggest, earned range. Varied calls, defensive reminders, a dramatic finish into the countdown.

## D. Session-support archetypes

### Recovery
- **Purpose:** active recovery / technique flow — low intensity, movement and form, sustainable training.
- **Duration:** 120–180s, generous rest.
- **Teaching objective:** stay loose, breathe, move smoothly, groove clean mechanics without strain.
- **Common mistakes:** turning it into a hard round, tensing up, rushing.
- **Coach behaviour:** calm, low, unhurried. Breathing and rhythm cues; comfortable with silence. Ideal for the Calm coach.

### Warmup (special round)
- **Purpose:** prepare *this session's* work — raise heart rate, loosen, prime the specific skills to come.
- **Duration:** one round, low intensity.
- **Teaching objective:** find range and rhythm; wake up the movements the workout will use.
- **Common mistakes:** generic warmup unrelated to the session; going too hard.
- **Coach behaviour:** patient, priming, sets the intent and tone for the whole workout.

### Cooldown (special round)
- **Purpose:** bring it down, reinforce the objective, close the session honestly.
- **Duration:** one round, low intensity.
- **Teaching objective:** slow the heart rate, groove the day's skill lightly, end with a sense of completion.
- **Common mistakes:** skipping it, or an abrupt stop with no close.
- **Coach behaviour:** slow, low, warm. Reinforces what was learned and delivers the honest close.

## E. Stance-specific archetypes

### Southpaw Specific
- **Purpose:** coach the southpaw's actual game — not an orthodox workout mirrored carelessly.
- **Duration:** 150–180s.
- **Teaching objective:** the lead-hand game, the straight left down the middle, foot positioning and angles vs. orthodox, owning the outside.
- **Common mistakes:** treating it as "orthodox but flipped," neglecting the foot-outside battle, straight left looping.
- **Coach behaviour:** stance-expert and identity-affirming (see the Southpaw coach). Cues are stance-aware and specific: "step outside the lead foot," "straight left on the line," "own the angle." Never generic.

> **Authoring note:** any archetype can be written stance-aware. A "Straight Right" for orthodox becomes the "Straight Left" for southpaw — but southpaw workouts must use genuinely southpaw *tactics and footwork*, not just swapped hand names.

## F. Composing rounds into a workout

- **Order for build:** development archetypes early → layering/defense in the middle → pressure/fight-pace/simulation late → recovery/cooldown to close.
- **Relate the rounds:** a punch introduced in a development round returns in a combination, then under pressure. That spiral is what teaches.
- **One focus per round** — even a Fight Simulation round has the focus "put it together," not "everything at once."
- **Match archetype to difficulty** — the same archetype (e.g. Defence) is coached with high density for beginners and sparse precision for advanced.
