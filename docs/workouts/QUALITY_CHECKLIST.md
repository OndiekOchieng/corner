# QUALITY_CHECKLIST.md — The Editorial Gate

No workout ships until it passes this review. Validation in `WORKOUT_SCHEMA.md §10` checks that a workout is *structurally* correct; this checklist checks that it's *good coaching*. A workout can be schema-valid and still fail here — and if it fails here, it is not a Corner workout.

**How to use it:** the author self-reviews against every item, then a second coach reviews independently. The most important review is the **read-aloud test** (§4).

---

## 1. Objective & structure

- [ ] **Clear single objective.** One sentence; the athlete leaves better at *one main thing*. Not a grab-bag.
- [ ] **The workout delivers on its objective.** The rounds actually build the thing the name/objective promises.
- [ ] **Warmup prepares the athlete** for *this* session's specific work (not a generic loosen-up).
- [ ] **Cooldown reinforces learning** and closes honestly (not an abrupt stop).
- [ ] **Every round has one focus**, and the rounds **relate and build** (a spiral, not interchangeable rounds).
- [ ] **Natural progression** — the session opens deliberately, builds, peaks late, and releases.
- [ ] **Difficulty matches the declared level** — content, complexity, and tone fit beginner/intermediate/advanced (`PROGRESSION_MODEL.md`).

## 2. Coaching quality

- [ ] **Correct cue density** — baseline ~one coaching cue per 20–40s of work, scaled to type/level. Not a firehose, not dead air.
- [ ] **No dead air** — no whole round of work left uncoached (unless a deliberate conditioning grind, still punctuated by breathing/pace).
- [ ] **No excessive talking** — no stacked cues, no filling silence, working room after every cue.
- [ ] **No contradictory coaching** — cues don't fight (e.g. "stay long" then "get inside" with no transition; "relax" then "explode" back-to-back with no arc).
- [ ] **One idea per cue** — no chained corrections.
- [ ] **Teaching only in rest** — longer explanation lives in the rest window, never mid-round.
- [ ] **Rest supports learning** — recovers the athlete, teaches the "why," and previews what's next.
- [ ] **Encouragement is earned and specific** — no generic hype on repeat.

## 3. Voice & safety (the trust rules)

- [ ] **Nothing mistimed** — especially the countdown and bell (exact). A mistimed cue fails the whole workout.
- [ ] **Nothing that claims to see the athlete** — universal reminders only, no fake observation.
- [ ] **Nothing unsafe** — no cue that could injure; pain is respected, never "pushed through."
- [ ] **Nothing that shames** — challenges, never demeans.
- [ ] **No robot/app/error speak** — the coach never breaks character.
- [ ] **Wording is boxing-true and varied** — real terms, and high-frequency cues rotate wordings (no looping).
- [ ] **Sentence length in range** — mid-round 1–6 words/one breath; teaching (rest) 1–3 sentences.

## 4. Hands-free & feel (the read-aloud test)

- [ ] **Read the entire workout aloud, in real time**, imagining an athlete on the bag who cannot see the screen. Does it feel like **coaching** or like a **timer with talking**?
- [ ] **Hands-free friendly** — the athlete never needs to look at or touch the screen to follow the session. Everything essential is spoken.
- [ ] **The athlete always knows what to do** — every moment has clear structure or a clear cue; no confusing gaps.
- [ ] **Energy tracks the arc** — calm open, steady middle, rising push, peak countdown, low rest, honest close. It *moves*.
- [ ] **It ends well** — the close is specific and satisfying; the athlete feels they *trained*.

## 5. Personality & fit

- [ ] **Coach personality is consistent** — the workout's voice matches its recommended coach pack(s) throughout; no tonal whiplash.
- [ ] **Recommended coach packs fit** — the suggested packs suit the workout's character (a Fight Conditioning session recommends Fight Night / Competition, not Calm).
- [ ] **Metadata complete and correct** — name, id, objective, difficulty, stance, type, tags, author, version, relationships (`WORKOUT_SCHEMA.md`).
- [ ] **It's unmistakably Corner** — if this workout could belong to a generic timer app, it fails. It should feel authored by a coach who cares.

---

## 6. Severity — what blocks a ship

**Blockers (must fix before ship):** no objective; missing warmup/cooldown; any mistimed/unsafe/shaming/see-the-athlete cue; contradictory coaching; cue firehose or dead air; difficulty mismatch; fails the read-aloud "feels like coaching" test.

**Must-fix-soon (ship only with a note + follow-up):** thin cue-wording variety; weak close; suboptimal round ordering; coach-pack fit slightly off.

**Polish (track, don't block):** a sharper phrasing available; a better tag; a stronger preview line.

---

## 7. The reviewer's question

For every workout, the reviewer answers one final question honestly:

> *"Would I be proud to run this session, exactly as written, with a real athlete on a real bag — and would they finish feeling coached by someone who cared?"*

If the answer isn't a confident yes, it isn't ready.
