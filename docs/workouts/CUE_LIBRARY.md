# CUE_LIBRARY.md — The Coaching Vocabulary

Cues are the words the coach says. This library is the **first shared vocabulary** so that every author writes cues that are correct, boxing-true, and unmistakably Corner — and so the same idea can be said many ways to avoid robotic repetition.

Governed by `../product/VOICE_GUIDELINES.md` (how it sounds) and `../product/COACHING_PHILOSOPHY.md` (when to say it). Read those first.

> A cue fixes **one thing**, in **one breath**, at the **right moment**, in **boxing language** — or it doesn't exist.

---

## 1. Global rules for every cue

- **Maximum length:** mid-round cues are **1–6 words, one breath.** Round intros are one short sentence. Only **rest** cues may run 1–3 sentences (the teaching window).
- **One idea per cue.** Never chain corrections ("hands up and turn your hip and move").
- **Boxing-specific and concrete.** Real terms, concrete verbs ("snap the jab," "sit down on the cross"), never vague ("do more," "go harder").
- **Never claim to see the athlete.** Universal reminders only ("hands back"), never fake observation ("your left is dropping").
- **Never mistimed, unsafe, or shaming.** (Hard rules — `COACHING_PHILOSOPHY.md §6`.)
- **Vary the wording.** Every idea below lists multiple phrasings. Rotate them so the coach never sounds like a looping recording. Repetition is the enemy of trust.

---

## 2. Cue density (how much to say)

- **Baseline:** one *coaching* cue per **20–40 seconds** of work, plus the fixed structure cues (round call, combination, countdown).
- **Scale by:** workout type (technical = denser; conditioning = sparse), athlete level (beginner = denser; advanced = sparse), and coach pack.
- **Hard limits:** never stack two cues within a few seconds; never leave a whole round of work with *no* coaching (dead air) unless it's a deliberate conditioning grind (and even then, breathing/pace cues punctuate it).

---

## 3. Escalation (energy across a round)

Cues escalate with the round's arc (`VOICE_GUIDELINES.md §5`):

```
open (calm, setup) → mid (steady, instructional) → final-30 (rising) → countdown (peak, clipped) → bell → rest (drop to low)
```

The *same* category's cues shift in energy: an early "stay relaxed" becomes a late "empty the tank."

---

## 4. Cue categories

For each: purpose, preferred wordings (rotate these), and words to avoid.

### Technique
- **Purpose:** sharpen mechanics of a punch/skill.
- **Preferred:** "Snap the jab." · "Sit down on the cross." · "Turn the hip." · "Tight on the hook." · "Same line — don't loop it." · "Short and straight."
- **Avoid:** vague ("better technique"), multi-part corrections, anatomy the athlete can't feel mid-round.

### Movement
- **Purpose:** footwork, angles, not standing still.
- **Preferred:** "Off the balls of your feet." · "Cut the angle." · "Jab and step." · "Don't be there — move." · "Stay light."
- **Avoid:** "move more" (vague), telling them to move *straight back* (bad habit).

### Guard
- **Purpose:** protect — the most-repeated reminder, so vary it hardest.
- **Preferred:** "Hands back." · "Chin down." · "Guard home." · "Recover the hands." · "Hands after you punch."
- **Avoid:** saying the exact same guard cue every time (looping) — rotate these relentlessly.

### Breathing
- **Purpose:** stay relaxed, don't gas, exhale on shots.
- **Preferred:** "Breathe with the shots." · "Long, easy breaths." · "Exhale on the punch." · "Don't hold your breath." · "Stay loose."
- **Avoid:** over-cueing breathing to the point of distraction.

### Power
- **Purpose:** put weight into shots, from the ground.
- **Preferred:** "Sit on it." · "Power from the floor." · "Drive off the back foot." · "Whip it, don't push it."
- **Avoid:** "punch harder" (invites arm-punching and imbalance), cueing power at the cost of guard.

### Rhythm
- **Purpose:** flow, timing, not robotic.
- **Preferred:** "Find your rhythm." · "Smooth and sharp." · "Punch in bunches, then reset." · "Don't force it."
- **Avoid:** metronomic counting that removes the athlete's own timing (unless drilling a specific tempo).

### Distance
- **Purpose:** range control — where the work happens.
- **Preferred:** "Find your range." · "Long on the jab." · "Step in, then off." · "Own the outside." · "Get to your distance."
- **Avoid:** contradicting the round's range intent (don't cue "get inside" in an outside-boxing round).

### Recovery
- **Purpose:** rest-period recovery guidance.
- **Preferred:** "Breathe deep, drop the shoulders." · "Shake it out." · "Recover — roll the neck." · "Slow the breath down."
- **Avoid:** hype during rest, counting the athlete down aggressively, filling the whole rest with talk.

### Mindset
- **Purpose:** focus, composure, intent — earned, sparse.
- **Preferred:** "Stay composed." · "Sharp mind, loose body." · "This is your round." · "Trust your work."
- **Avoid:** generic hype ("you got this!" on repeat), anything that shames.

### Urgency
- **Purpose:** the push — final 30s, last round.
- **Preferred:** "Last thirty — empty it out." · "Dig now." · "Finish strong." · "This is where rounds are won." · "Everything you've got."
- **Avoid:** urgency too early (nothing left for the real push), shouting into unintelligibility, urgency in a recovery round.

### Ring IQ
- **Purpose:** tactics — the thinking fighter's cues.
- **Preferred:** "Set it up behind the jab." · "Cut the ring off." · "Feint, then go." · "Make him miss, make him pay." · "Control the center."
- **Avoid:** tactics beyond the athlete's level (advanced Ring IQ in a beginner workout), fake observation of an opponent that isn't there — frame as *your* actions ("cut the angle"), not the opponent's.

## 5. Structure cues (fixed, non-negotiable wording discipline)

These occupy fixed moments; keep them crisp and consistent (athletes rely on them):

- **Round call:** "Round three. Body work." (round number + focus)
- **Combination call:** "Jab, cross, hook." (named punches, ahead of the beat)
- **Countdown:** "Ten seconds." … "Five. Four. Three. Two. One." (exact — the most trust-critical lines in the app)
- **Rest call:** "Rest." + one recovery cue + a preview of next.
- **Finish:** an honest, specific close (not generic "great job").

---

## 6. Words & habits to avoid (global blacklist)

- Empty hype on repeat: "you got this," "amazing," "crushing it," "beast."
- Vague intensity: "do more," "harder," "faster" without a *how*.
- Fake observation: "your hand is dropping," "nice head movement" (can't see it).
- Shaming: "slow," "weak," "pathetic," "lazy" *(as an insult — "don't get lazy" as a challenge is fine)*.
- Medical claims: "you're fine," "push through the pain."
- App/error/robot speak mid-workout: "loading," "as an AI," "error."
- Looping the identical cue — rotate wordings.
- Talking to fill silence.

---

## 7. Anti-repetition principle

Because a cue like "hands back" may be needed many times, **every high-frequency idea must ship with 4+ interchangeable wordings**, and authors (and coach packs) rotate them. A coach who says the exact same sentence the exact same way every 30 seconds stops being a coach and becomes a recording — and the athlete stops listening. Variety *is* trust.

---

## 8. Amendment — PR-020C: dimensions, reinforcement & call signs

- **Every cue has a teaching dimension** (guard, footwork, distance, breathing, rhythm,
  power, head, output, …), classified from its wording. The first cue of a dimension in
  a round is spoken **verbatim**; later same-dimension cues are **reinforced** in fresh
  words by the runtime (see [COACHING_MEMORY.md](../coaching/COACHING_MEMORY.md)). Authors
  still write the authoritative first line; the anti-repetition burden for reminders is
  now shared with the runtime.
- **Combination cues may carry call-sign metadata** — an optional punch-number sequence
  (`[1, 2, 6]`) so the coach can voice the combo at each pack's vocabulary level and
  teach the numbers naturally (see [BOXING_LEXICON.md](../coaching/BOXING_LEXICON.md)).
  Authored cue *text* is still spoken verbatim; the metadata only enables coach-voiced
  call signs.
