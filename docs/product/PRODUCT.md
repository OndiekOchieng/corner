# PRODUCT.md — What Corner Is

> "The coach in your corner."

This is the source of truth for what Corner is trying to become. Every workout, screen, voice line, and feature decision should trace back to this document. If a proposed feature does not serve the mission below, it does not belong in Corner.

---

## 1. Mission

**Give every person who hits a heavy bag a real coach — a voice in their corner that teaches, times, and pushes them — without them ever touching a screen.**

## 2. Vision

A world where you can walk up to a heavy bag anywhere — a garage, a hotel gym, a spare bedroom — put your phone down, and get a coached boxing workout as good as a session with a trainer who knows what they're doing. Not a timer that beeps. A coach that *coaches*.

Over time, Corner becomes the coach you choose: different personalities, different styles, different fighters' philosophies — all speaking through the same trustworthy timing and structure.

## 3. Target users

Corner is built for people who **train boxing on a bag, mostly alone**:

- **The returning boxer** — trained years ago, has a bag at home, wants structure and someone to call the rounds and combinations.
- **The self-taught enthusiast** — learned from YouTube, can throw punches, but has no one to correct pace, guard, or footwork.
- **The fitness boxer** — comes for the workout and the stress relief, stays for feeling like a fighter.
- **The traveling athlete** — trains wherever there's a bag, needs their coach to travel with them.

They share three things: **they have a bag (or shadowbox), they train without a partner, and they want to feel coached, not just timed.**

Corner is **not** primarily for: absolute beginners with no bag and no space (they need video demonstration first), competitive fighters with a real full-time coach (Corner supplements, never replaces), or people who want a generic HIIT timer.

## 4. Primary promise

> **Press start. Put the phone down. Get coached through a real boxing workout — hands-free, start to finish.**

The single measurable proof of this promise: **the athlete does not touch the screen between the first bell and the final bell.** If they reach for the phone mid-workout, Corner has failed its core promise.

## 5. Core principles

1. **Voice first, screen last.** The primary interface is the coach's voice. The screen is a glance, not a gaze. If a feature requires looking at the screen mid-round, redesign it.
2. **The coach earns trust.** Timing must be dead accurate, cues must land at the right moment, and the coach must never say anything wrong. Trust is the whole product; one mistimed or nonsensical cue erodes it.
3. **Silence is coaching.** A great coach doesn't narrate constantly. Corner speaks with purpose and shuts up when you should be working. (See `COACHING_PHILOSOPHY.md`.)
4. **Structure over novelty.** Real boxing workouts have shape: warm up, work rounds, rest, cool down. Corner respects the craft. It is not a random-combination generator.
5. **Coach, don't cheerlead.** Motivation is earned and specific, not a constant stream of "you got this." (See `VOICE_GUIDELINES.md`.)
6. **Meet the athlete where they are.** A beginner and an experienced fighter should not get the same workout or the same coaching density.
7. **Personality matters.** People choose coaches they connect with. Corner's coach packs are personalities, not skins. (See `COACH_PACKS.md`.)

## 6. Success metrics

Corner is succeeding when:

| Metric | What it proves | Target signal |
|---|---|---|
| **Hands-free completion** — % of workouts finished with zero screen touches after start | The core promise | Most sessions have 0 mid-workout touches |
| **Completion rate** — % of started workouts finished | Workouts are the right length/intensity, coach keeps them in it | High and rising |
| **Coach trust** (survey) — "Did the coach ever say something wrong or mistimed?" | Timing + cue quality | Approaching "never" |
| **Return rate** — sessions per athlete per week over time | Corner became a habit, not a novelty | Sustained weekly use |
| **Coach clarity** (survey) — "Did you always know what to do?" | Cue quality + voice design | Consistently high |
| **Enjoyment / "felt coached"** (survey) | The emotional payoff | High; distinguishes us from timers |

Explicitly **not** primary metrics: session length maximization, feature count, screens-per-session, notification-driven re-engagement. Corner wins by being *good in the gym*, not sticky on the couch.

## 7. What Corner IS

- A **boxing coach** that runs a structured, voiced workout on a heavy bag or in shadowbox.
- **Hands-free** by design — voice-led, glanceable, phone-down.
- A **teacher** — it calls combinations, corrects pace, reminds you of guard and footwork, and explains during rest.
- A set of **coach personalities** you can choose between.
- **Trustworthy timing** — the bell, the rounds, and the countdown are exact.
- **Resumable and personal** — it remembers where you were and what you've done.

## 8. What Corner is NOT

- **Not an interval timer.** Timers beep. Corner coaches. A timer tells you *when*; Corner tells you *what*, *how*, and *why*, and knows when to be quiet.
- **Not a HIIT/circuit app.** Corner is boxing-specific — stance, combinations, defense, footwork, ring craft.
- **Not a video course.** Corner assumes you can already throw a punch (or is honest when it can't teach that hands-free). It coaches the *work*, not a lecture.
- **Not a cheerleader.** It won't drown you in generic hype.
- **Not a screen you stare at.** No mid-round dashboards demanding attention.
- **Not a social/gamification platform.** No feeds, streaks-as-manipulation, or leaderboards as the point. (A private history that reflects real work is fine; competition-as-engagement-hook is not.)
- **Not a replacement for a real coach** if you have one — it's the coach for when you don't.

---

## 9. The one-sentence differentiator

Every other bag-workout app is a **timer with a workout list**. Corner is a **coach with a voice you trust and a personality you choose** — and the proof is that you never pick up your phone.
