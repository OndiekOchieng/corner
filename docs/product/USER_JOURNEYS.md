# USER_JOURNEYS.md — The Athlete's Experience

These are the moments that make or break Corner, told from the athlete's point of view — what they *do*, what they *hear*, what they *see*, and what they *feel*. Every journey is measured against the primary promise: **press start, put the phone down, get coached — hands-free.**

Design lens for all journeys: **the screen is a glance; the voice is the experience.** If a journey requires sustained looking at the screen, it's wrong.

---

## 1. First launch (the first 60 seconds decide everything)

**Goal:** the athlete understands "this is a coach, not a timer" and is throwing punches within a minute — without a tutorial wall.

- **Sees:** a calm, dark, uncluttered screen. Corner's name and promise. Not a settings maze, not a signup wall, not a feature tour.
- **Does:** picks a coach (or gets a sensible default) and a first workout suited to a newcomer. One or two taps.
- **Hears (on start):** the coach introduces itself and the session with warmth and intent — establishing the relationship in the first breath.
- **Feels:** *"Oh — this is a person in my corner, not an app."* Intrigued, not overwhelmed.

**Friction to kill:** long onboarding, jargon before trust, forcing account creation, choice paralysis over workouts. **First-launch success = throwing punches to a coached round within ~60 seconds.**

## 2. Starting the first workout

**Goal:** prove the promise immediately.

- **Sees:** the workout's name, intent, duration, rounds — a glanceable pre-flight so the athlete knows what they're in for. One clear **Start**.
- **Does:** presses Start, **puts the phone down / hangs it up, and steps to the bag.**
- **Hears:** intro → warmup → the bell → round one, coached. The coach calls what to throw, corrects, counts down, calls rest. All hands-free.
- **Feels:** trust building with every well-timed cue. The moment the countdown lands *exactly* right, they believe.

**The make-or-break instant:** the first countdown and first bell. If timing is perfect, trust is won and they never touch the phone. If it's off, they reach for the screen — and the promise is broken.

## 3. The returning athlete

**Goal:** make coming back frictionless and make the athlete feel *known*.

- **Sees:** home reflects them — their coach, a suggested next workout based on what they've done, their recent history. Not a generic catalog every time.
- **Does:** one tap to start today's session (or pick another).
- **Hears:** their chosen coach, familiar — the relationship persists. Optionally a light acknowledgment of continuity ("Back at it — let's build on the jab work").
- **Feels:** continuity and belonging. This is *their* coach and *their* training, not a fresh anonymous session each time.

**Principle:** returning should feel like walking back into your gym, not opening an app.

## 4. Interruption (real life happens mid-workout)

**Goal:** never punish the athlete for an interruption — a phone call, the doorbell, needing water, the screen sleeping.

- **What happens:** the athlete stops, or the app is backgrounded, or the device locks mid-round.
- **The athlete's expectation:** their session isn't lost. When they come back, they can pick up where they were.
- **Sees (on return):** clear state — where they were, and an obvious way to **Resume** or end.
- **Hears (on resume):** the coach continues from that point — **not** a replay of the intro and rounds already done. No duplicated speech, no phantom bells.
- **Feels:** relief and trust. "It held my place. It didn't make me start over or listen to everything again."

**Non-negotiables:** resume continues cleanly (the platform is built for this); the athlete is never forced to re-do completed rounds or re-hear past coaching. An abandoned session is remembered, not silently discarded.

## 5. Resume (the trust test)

**Goal:** resuming feels seamless and *correct*.

- **Does:** taps Resume.
- **Hears:** picks up mid-round exactly where the work left off — the current round's remaining time, the coaching from here forward. No re-intro, no double bell, no "round one" again.
- **Sees:** the live round state, glanceable, then puts the phone back down.
- **Feels:** the coach *kept the athlete's place* like a real trainer would. This is a moment that deepens trust rather than testing patience.

**Guardrail:** if a resume can't be done cleanly, it is better to offer a clear, honest choice ("resume near where you were" or "start fresh") than to fake it or replay history.

## 6. Workout completion (the payoff)

**Goal:** the athlete finishes feeling they *trained* — worked, coached, and acknowledged.

- **Hears (final round → bell):** the coach's biggest, earned energy, then release — an honest, specific close. Not generic "great job," but "solid work — the jab got sharper in those middle rounds. Good session."
- **Sees:** a simple, satisfying summary — what they did (rounds, time), a chance to note how it felt (rating/notes), nothing demanding.
- **Does:** catches their breath. Optionally rates the session. Closes.
- **Feels:** accomplished and *seen*. The emotional dividend of "I trained today, with my coach."

**Principle:** the close is the emotional product. A timer just stops. A coach *finishes* with you.

## 7. History (a record of real work)

**Goal:** a private, honest reflection of training — motivation through evidence, not manipulation.

- **Sees:** past sessions — what, when, how it felt. A record they can be quietly proud of. Simple. Not a feed, not a leaderboard, not a manipulative streak-guilt machine.
- **Does:** glances at progress, maybe revisits a workout they liked.
- **Feels:** momentum. "Look what I've put in." Evidence of consistency is the honest motivator.

**Non-goal:** gamification-as-hook. History reflects reality; it doesn't manufacture engagement.

## 8. Daily use (the habit)

**Goal:** Corner becomes part of how the athlete trains — sustainable, not a novelty that burns out.

- **The loop:** open → one tap → phone down → coached session → honest close → done. Minimal friction on both ends.
- **Variety with continuity:** enough range (workout types, difficulty, coach packs) to stay fresh; enough consistency (their coach, their progress) to feel like a practice.
- **Room for light days:** recovery and technique-flow sessions mean showing up lightly still counts — which is what makes a habit survive.
- **Feels:** "this is my training." Corner is the reason the bag gets used, not another app that gets opened once.

**The habit test:** does the athlete reach for Corner because they want to *train*, not because a notification nagged them?

---

## 9. The through-line

Across every journey, the same truths hold:

1. **Hands-free is sacred** — no journey should force sustained screen time mid-workout.
2. **The coach is a relationship** — persistent, personal, remembered.
3. **Trust is the product** — timing, resume, and the honest close all protect it.
4. **The athlete is respected** — never nagged, shamed, manipulated, or made to start over.

If a design decision violates one of these, it's the wrong decision — no matter how good it looks on the screen.
