# Discovery — Who is the Coach?

**Product discovery only. No implementation, no personalities, no prompts, no dialogue.** The
deliverable is *understanding* — a proposed answer to the most fundamental question Corner had
not yet asked. It builds on the accepted philosophy
([PHILOSOPHY_REFRESH](../PHILOSOPHY_REFRESH.md), [COACHING_DOCTRINE](COACHING_DOCTRINE.md),
[PERSONALITY_SYSTEM](PERSONALITY_SYSTEM.md)) and proposes; it does not amend them.

> Not *"what words should he say?"* Not *"what should Coach Alpha sound like?"* The question is:
> **who is standing in the athlete's corner?**

---

## Verdict

**PARTIALLY — and a reframe: the Coach is a *relationship*, not a personality.**

Two things are true at once:

1. **What belongs to *every* Coach is already answered.** Corner's accepted philosophy has,
   over a month of discovery, fully defined the invariants every coach inherits (below). That
   half is settled.
2. **What makes each Coach a distinct *relationship* is not.** Today the packs vary the
   *surface* — voice, vocabulary, timing texture, framing ("the same boxing, a different human")
   — but whether they offer a genuinely different *relationship* (not just a different voice) is
   under-explored. And there is an unresolved tension between *distinguishability* and
   *immersion* that future personality work must face head-on.

So the reframe: we kept treating the Coach as a *personality* (how he sounds). He is a
**relationship** (who he is *to the athlete*). Personality is what varies; relationship is what
the Coach *is*.

---

## 1. What a Coach *is* in Corner (deliverable §1)

> **A Coach is the athlete's corner — made audible.** Not a voice, not a personality, not a set
> of prompts: a *someone* who stands in the corner, exists entirely for the athlete's boxing, is
> **present without being the protagonist**, **honest without claiming to see**, and
> **continuous** (never stops). The personality is how that relationship *feels*; the
> relationship is what the Coach *is*.

Corner already crossed from "voice pack" to "a human." This is the next step: from *a human* to
*a relationship*. A human is a thing that exists; a relationship is a thing that exists **for
someone**. The Coach only exists in the second person.

---

## 2. The four owners (deliverables §2, §3, §4 + questions 2–5)

The prompt's "must-survive" list is real, but it does not all belong to the *Coach*. Sorting it
by owner is the discovery:

| Owner | What it owns | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Corner** (the ground every coach stands on) | the platform promises + the world | Time (Engine) · transitions (Bell) · the athlete is the north star · *forget the phone, remember the boxing* · behaviour over information · success is an athlete experience |
| **Every Coach** (the invariants that survive *every* coach) | the relationship's non-negotiables | **The Coach never stops coaching** · perfect timing · **never claim to see the athlete** · never shame/unsafe/break character · silence is the default · the same correct boxing · **always for the athlete, never the protagonist** |
| **Personality** (what may vary) | how the relationship *feels* | tone · vocabulary · pacing · humour · intensity · encouragement · technical density · *how* silence is used · **what the athlete feels** (dangerous / composed / technical / calm) |
| **The athlete** | the point of it all | the boxing · the experience · whether they forgot the phone · what the corner meant to them |

So, answering the prompt's placements:
- *Continuous Coaching · the athlete is the north star · forget the phone · Bell philosophy · the
  Coach never stops coaching* — these belong to **Corner and every Coach**, **not** to
  personalities. A personality that broke any of them would not be a Corner coach.
- **Question 2 — would changing personality change the boxing?** No. The boxing is identical
  across coaches (PERSONALITY_SYSTEM). **Question 3 — would it change the philosophy?** No — the
  invariants are inherited, not chosen. **Question 4 — personality or relationship?** A
  **relationship**; the personality is its surface. **Question 1 — what makes one Coach
  different?** Not the words — the *relationship* they offer, of which tone/vocabulary/feeling
  are the visible edge.

---

## 3. The relationship (deliverable — who serves whom)

```
        The Athlete            (the north star — the protagonist)
             │  serves…            ▲  … is served by
             ▼                     │
        The Boxing  ◄──────────  The Coach   (exists entirely for the athlete)
        (the point)
```

**Who exists to serve whom?** The Coach serves the athlete; the athlete serves *their own
boxing*. The boxing is the point; the athlete is the north star; the Coach is the servant. Stated
plainly, and inviolable: **the Coach exists for the athlete; the athlete does not exist for the
Coach.**

---

## 4. Emotions — what should the athlete feel? (deliverable)

There is an **invariant** feeling and a **variable** one:

- **Invariant (every Coach must produce):** *I have someone in my corner.* Accompanied. In good
  hands. Trusted, never watched. And — the ceiling of all of it — *immersed: I forgot the phone.*
- **Variable (the personality's signature):** *which* someone — dangerous, composed, technical,
  calm. This is what one Coach makes an athlete feel that another does not.

A Coach who nails the variable but fails the invariant (a vivid voice that leaves the athlete
feeling alone or watched) has failed. The invariant is the floor; the personality is the colour.

---

## 5. The North Star test (deliverable)

> If the bell rings and the athlete closes their eyes, who walked into the corner?

- A **different voice** → **failure** (a skin).
- A **different personality** → better.
- A **different relationship** → **the target.** You would know a specific *someone* was there —
  not because they announced themselves, but because of *how the corner felt.*

And the **wrong-question corner**: during the round, the athlete should not be wondering *who* is
coaching them at all — they should be **boxing**. So the truest success is a Coach who is
**distinguishable in retrospect, invisible in the moment.** Afterwards the athlete could say
"that was the old-school one." *During*, they never thought about the coach — they thought about
the bag. Distinguishability serves *choosing* a coach; immersion serves *training* with one.
These are not the same axis, and future work must hold both.

---

## 6. What no Coach must ever become (deliverable)

Each of these is already partly enforced by the doctrine (PR-028/030) — naming them as
*relationship* failures makes them permanent:

| Never | Because |
|---|---|
| The protagonist | the athlete is the north star |
| More important than the athlete | the Coach exists *for* the athlete |
| An interruption | *forget the phone* — a coach who makes you remember it has failed |
| A narrator | a coach coaches *behaviour*; he does not *describe* the workout |
| A timer | the Bell and the Engine own time; a coach who counts is software |
| A collection of prompts | a relationship is not a template — the moment it reads as prompts, the someone is gone |

---

## 7. Would an athlete know who coached them after one workout? (question 7)

**Partially, today.** PR-028 gave the six packs genuinely distinct registers, so the *surface*
differs. Whether the *relationship* differs — whether closing your eyes reveals a different
*someone* and not just a different *voice* — is exactly the open question. And note: **that is
not the primary goal.** The primary goal is that the athlete forgot the phone. Distinguishability
is a *secondary* good (it lets an athlete re-choose their corner), and it must never be bought at
the cost of immersion.

## 8. Has Corner's philosophy already answered some of this? (question 8)

**Yes — most of it.** *What belongs to Corner and every Coach* is settled by the accepted
philosophy. What remains genuinely open is narrow: **what makes each Coach a distinct
relationship**, and **how to hold distinguishability and immersion together.** The invariants are
done; the differentiation is not.

---

## Recommendations — future Coach Personality discovery ONLY (deliverable §5)

For the personality work that this unblocks (its own discovery, not this one):

1. **Start from the relationship, not the voice.** Ask of each pack: *what kind of corner
   presence is this?* (the gym elder who's seen everything; the fight-night believer; the calm
   technician). Derive the feeling from the relationship, and only *then* the words — words are
   the last, thinnest layer (PR-028 already proved fewer words win).
2. **Measure by the athlete's felt relationship, not by transcript distinguishability.** *"Did I
   feel a specific someone was in my corner?"* — the athlete lens, not a developer diffing two
   scripts. (Athlete UAT ≠ developer investigation — accepted philosophy.)
3. **Resolve distinguishable-vs-invisible explicitly.** Define personality success as
   *distinguishable in retrospect, invisible in the moment* — never as *noticeable mid-round.*
4. **Every Coach inherits the invariants unchanged.** Personality varies the surface and the
   feeling; it never touches the promises (never stops coaching · silence · trust · never the
   protagonist · the athlete is the north star).
5. **Do not begin with prompts or dialogue.** A Coach is a relationship; a prompt library is its
   corpse. If personality discovery starts by writing lines, it has already answered the wrong
   question.

None of the above is done here. This discovery only *proposes the frame* future work should use.

---

## The final answer

> Who walked into the athlete's corner today?

**Someone who exists entirely for the athlete's boxing** — present, honest, silent by default,
continuous, and never the protagonist. *That* he is such a someone is **the Coach**, and it is
already settled and shared by every coach. *Which* such someone — dangerous, composed, calm — is
the **personality**, and it is the surface of a deeper thing we have not yet discovered: the
**relationship** each coach offers.

**PARTIALLY**, then: Corner knows what every Coach *must be*; it has not yet discovered what makes
each Coach a distinct *relationship*. And we had been asking a shallower question — *what should
the Coach sound like?* — when the real one is *who is he to the athlete?* Answer that, and the
words take care of themselves.

**No code, no personalities, no prompts. The deliverable is understanding.**
