# FIRST_COACH_PERFORMANCE_PACK.md — One Workout, Six Coaches

This is the proof that the Coach Performance System works: **one workout, performed six ways.** The workout events are *identical* across all six — same rounds, same durations, same countdown and bell, same skills taught. Only the coach's performance changes: the voice, the timing texture, the humour, the framing, the silences.

The workout is **Footwork Essentials** (`../workouts/FIRST_WORKOUT_PACK.md §1`) — the entry-point session, Beginner, 4×(120/60): Warmup → Footwork → Jab and Move → Footwork (angles) → Cooldown.

Read alongside `PERSONALITY_SYSTEM.md` (the six coaches) and `ROUND_DIRECTING.md` (the arc every version follows). Lines below are representative performance, not a full script; `[…work…]` marks a deliberate silence where the athlete works.

> Same fight, six corners. If you can hear six different people in what follows — while the boxing stays identical — the system is real.

---

## The fixed skeleton (identical for every coach)

Every version delivers exactly this. Nothing structural moves.

| Segment | Time | Fixed events |
|---|---|---|
| Warmup | 120s | intent-setting intro · light movement · find range |
| R1 Footwork | 120/60 | round call · balance & stance cues · **countdown · bell** · rest (recover · teach · preview) |
| R2 Jab and Move | 120/60 | round call · jab-and-move cues · **countdown · bell** · rest |
| R3 Footwork (angles) | 120/60 | round call · cut-angles cues · **countdown · bell** · rest |
| Cooldown | 120s | slow movement · reinforce balance · honest close |

The countdown ("Ten seconds." … "Five. Four. Three. Two. One.") and the bell are **exact in every version** — no coach ever flexes those. What follows changes everything *around* that spine.

## What changed, and what didn't

**Identical across all six:** the five segments and their order; every duration; every countdown and bell (exact, always); the skills taught (balance, stance, jab-and-move, cutting angles); the arc of each round (open → develop → push → countdown → rest).

**Different in every one:**
- **Vocabulary & framing** — "the engine of everything" (Technical) vs. "feet, everything starts here" (Old School) vs. "footwork wins fights" (Fight Night).
- **Timing texture** — Calm and Southpaw leave long silences and quiet rests; Fight Night and Competition fill the peaks with more energy.
- **Intensity & dynamics** — Calm never rises above "easy"; Fight Night swings from low to roaring; Competition holds a demanding bar throughout.
- **Humour, temperament, and the three framing moments** — each intro, each rest, each close is unmistakably one person.
- **Tactical lens** — the Southpaw coach reframes the *same footwork events* around the foot battle and the angle, without changing a single event.

If you read these aloud and hear six coaches — while an engineer diffing the event timeline sees no difference at all — the Coach Performance System has done its job: **the performance is the product, layered cleanly on top of an unchanged workout.**
